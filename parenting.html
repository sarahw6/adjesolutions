Parenting Coaching

Raising resilient children in an unpredictable world

How do I prepare my children for a future I can't even predict? In today's world of shifting global politics, rapid AI advancement, and economic whiplash, many parents are asking themselves this question. I know I am. Sharing lessons from my experience of constantly moving and raising five multicultural third-culture children across Africa and turning to my husband, Eusebe Adje (goes by 'Adje'), for his generational 'tough love' wisdom from Benin where he grew up, I wrote my second book titled DIPLOMATICALLY 2: Django Parenting, A Guide for Raising Resilient Third-Culture Children.

I identified the 23 Django Parenting for Resilience Principles™ for raising hardworking, self-sufficient children who are prepared for any challenge in life. Adje's childhood nickname was 'Django' (meaning 'the wild one') and this parenting style transformed him from a rebellious boy into a disciplined man of faith.

We welcome candid conversations with you about the parenting challenges you may be facing. We are best placed to advise parents of younger children (12 and under). We do not claim to have all the answers, but we promise to share with you what's worked for us and to help you come up with what will work for you.
